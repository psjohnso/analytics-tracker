Getting Started

Logging In

The Analytics Project Tracker uses ArcGIS Online OAuth 2.0 authentication. When you first open the application, you'll be redirected to the City of Tucson ArcGIS Online portal to sign in with your organizational credentials.

After successful authentication, your name appears in the upper-right corner. Your session lasts 120 minutes, after which you'll be prompted to re-authenticate.

User Roles

  • Standard User: Can view all tabs (Overview, Projects, Tasks, Resources, Forecast), create and edit projects and tasks, edit allocation percentages, and submit project ideas.
  • Admin: Members of the designated Team Leads group gain access to the Settings tab and can promote project ideas to active status. An Admin badge appears next to your name.

Browser Requirements

The application runs entirely in the browser as a single HTML page. It works with modern versions of Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and Safari. No software installation is required.

Navigating the Application

Tab Bar

The top navigation bar provides access to the main sections:

  • Overview: Dashboard with KPI summary cards showing project counts by status, active tasks, and team allocation overview.
  • Projects: Full list of analytics projects with filtering, sorting, and detail views. Supports grid and list layouts.
  • Tasks: Individual task management with status tracking, due dates, and assignee information.
  • Resources: Team capacity planning with per-person allocation charts, utilization metrics, and an interactive allocation editor.
  • 📊 Forecast: Forward-looking capacity projection showing team availability and utilization trends.
  • ⚙️ Settings: (Admin only) Team member management and absence/time-off editing.

Sidebar Filters

The left sidebar provides filtering options that are always visible regardless of the active tab. Filter by project status, priority, category, partner department, ITD team, or team member. Task status filters are also available for narrowing task results. Active filters appear as highlighted chips — click to toggle. The content area updates immediately.

View Toggle

On the Projects and Tasks tabs, switch between Grid (card-based) and List (table-based) views using the toggle buttons in the toolbar. Grid is the default.

Toolbar Actions

  • 🔄 Refresh: Reloads all data from ArcGIS Online, including projects, tasks, and resource allocations.
  • 💡 Submit Idea: Opens a simplified form for submitting new project ideas. Ideas enter with "Idea" status and must be promoted by an Admin.
  • 📋 Weekly Review: Filters tasks to show only those with activity in the current week.

Understanding Calculations

Throughout the application, you'll see small ⓘ icons next to calculated values like utilization percentages, capacity hours, and availability dates. Click any of these icons to see a brief popup explaining how that value is calculated, including the formula used. Where applicable, the popup includes a "Learn more" link that opens the relevant section of this guide for a more detailed explanation.

Overview Tab

The Overview tab provides a high-level dashboard of the team's current workload. It displays summary KPI cards showing the number of projects by status (Active, On Hold, Complete, etc.), task counts, and overall team utilization. This tab hides the sidebar and view toggle to provide a clean dashboard layout.

My Work Tab (Logged-In Users)

The My Work tab provides a personalized dashboard that shows only your projects, tasks, and allocations. It appears in the tab bar after you sign in, and matches your ArcGIS Online display name to project leads, team members, and task assignees.

At-a-Glance KPIs

Five cards at the top show your active project count, open task count, overdue tasks, tasks due this week, and your current week utilization percentage. Overdue and due-this-week counts highlight in red and yellow respectively when they need attention.

My Week

Directly below the KPIs, this section shows your allocation breakdown for the current week from the Resources data. It displays your work schedule type (5/8, 4/10, or 9/80), which pay period week you're in (A or B), your scheduled hours, and your RDO day if applicable. A daily bar shows your scheduled hours and work window (e.g., "8h / 7a–5p") for each day of the current week (Mon–Fri), with days off marked as "OFF." Below that, it shows hours and percentage allocated to each project, with a visual bar chart, plus your total allocated hours, capacity, and remaining availability.

Two-Column Layout

Below Attention Needed, the remaining sections are arranged in two columns. The left column shows your Projects, while the right column shows your Tasks.

Attention Needed

This section proactively surfaces items that need your action, sorted by urgency:

  • Overdue tasks and projects (🔴 red) — Open tasks (In Progress, On Hold, or Waiting for Response) and active projects (Active, On Hold, or Waiting for Response) past their working due date. If a working due date has been set, that is used; otherwise the original due/end date is used. When the working due date differs from the original, the original date is shown in parentheses.
  • Due this week (🟡 yellow) — Open tasks and active projects with working due dates in the next 7 days.
  • Incomplete project data — Any project (except Complete/Canceled) where key fields are missing (description, end date, category, partner department, or problem statement).
  • Incomplete task data — Any task (except Complete/Canceled) that is missing a description, due date, or category.
  • Stale projects (⏳) — Active, On Hold, or Waiting for Response projects that haven't been edited in over 14 days. Shows the number of days since the last update and the date of the last change.
  • Ready to start (🚀) — Future or Scheduled projects whose start date has passed. These should be reviewed and moved to Active status.

Click any item to open its detail page.

Timeline (Gantt Chart)

A visual timeline showing your active, on-hold, and scheduled projects as horizontal bars spanning from start date to working due date. Tasks are nested under their parent project with thinner bars. The chart includes month headers for orientation, a red "TODAY" marker showing the current date, and red outlines on any bars that are past their due date. Click a project or task label to open its detail page. The chart scrolls horizontally if the timeline spans a wide range.

My Projects

All projects where you're the project lead or a listed team member. By default, only Active, Scheduled, and Idea projects are shown. Click "Show more" to reveal On Hold, Future, Complete, and Canceled projects. Each row shows task completion progress, and projects where you are the lead are marked with a "Lead" badge.

My Tasks

All tasks assigned to you. By default, only "In Progress" and "Waiting for Response" tasks are shown — these are the ones that need active attention. Name matching: The My Work tab matches your ArcGIS Online display name to the data. If your projects or tasks don't appear, ensure your display name in ArcGIS Online matches the name used in the project/task records (e.g., "Peter Johnson" not "pjohnson").

Time Tracking

Time tracking is an opt-in feature that must be enabled per employee by a team lead in the Settings tab. When enabled, a "⏱️ Time Tracking" panel appears in the My Work tab between the KPI cards and the My Week section.

How It Works

The system uses a start/stop timer model. To log time on a task:

  • Select a task from the dropdown (shows your In Progress, Waiting for Response, and On Hold tasks).
  • Click "▶ Start" to begin the timer.
  • Work on the task. The elapsed time updates automatically.
  • Click "⏸ Pause" to temporarily stop the timer — the entry is saved, and a green "▶" Resume button appears in today's log so you can quickly restart the same task later.
  • Click "⏹ Stop" when you're completely done with a work session. Hours are calculated automatically from the start and end times.

You can run multiple timers simultaneously on different tasks. Active timers display with a green pulsing indicator and a live elapsed time counter. Pausing and resuming a task creates separate time entries that sum to the correct total — there's no limit to how many times you can pause and resume.

Overnight and weekend protection: If you forget to stop a timer, the system automatically caps the logged hours to your actual work schedule. For example, if you start a timer at 3:00 PM and stop it at 9:00 AM the next morning, it only counts 3:00–5:00 PM on day one and 8:00–9:00 AM on day two (based on your configured work hours), minus lunch — not the raw 18-hour span. Weekends and RDO days are excluded entirely.

Today's Log

Completed time entries for today appear below the timer controls, showing the task name, start/end times, and calculated hours. You can:

  • ✏️ Edit: Adjust start/end times and add notes (useful if you forgot to start or stop the timer).
  • 🗑 Delete: Remove an incorrect entry.

Daily and weekly hour totals are displayed above the log.

Enabling Time Tracking

In the Settings tab, the team members table includes a ⏱️ checkbox column. Check the box to enable time tracking for that employee; uncheck to disable it. Changes take effect immediately. Time entries already recorded are preserved even if tracking is disabled — they can be re-enabled at any time.

Pilot approach: Consider enabling time tracking for a few team members first to test the workflow, then roll it out to the full team once the process is established.

AI Task Suggestions

On the project detail page, click the "✨ Suggest Tasks" button in the Tasks section to generate AI-powered task suggestions. The system sends your project's title, category, size, problem statement, description, deliverables, data sources, technical requirements, and team members to Claude, which returns 6-10 specific, actionable tasks tailored to your project.

Each suggestion shows a title, description, recommended category, tool, priority, and suggested assignee. You can:

  • Add individual tasks to create them one at a time
  • Add All to create all remaining suggestions at once
  • Close the panel without adding any tasks

Suggestions avoid duplicating existing tasks on the project. The more detail you provide in the project fields (especially deliverables, data sources, and problem statement), the more specific and useful the suggestions will be.

Project Statuses

Projects follow this lifecycle:

Idea → Active → On Hold / Complete / Canceled

The "Future" status is also available for planned work not yet started.

Project & Task Dates

Both projects and tasks use a three-date model to track schedule changes over time:

  • Original End / Due Date — Set when the project or task is first created. This is the baseline commitment. For projects, this field locks permanently once the project status changes to Active — it cannot be edited after that point. This preserves the original timeline for comparison.
  • Working Due Date — The current target completion date. Updated as scope or timelines shift. All overdue calculations, alerts, and deadline displays throughout the app use this date. On creation, it is automatically set to match the Original End Date if left blank.
  • Actual End Date — Automatically set to today's date when the status changes to "Complete." Represents when the work was actually finished.

Detail pages show all three dates side by side, with a red (moved) indicator when the working due date differs from the original. List views and cards show the working due date since that's the current target.

Roles and Auto-Fill

Each project row in the allocation editor includes a role dropdown (Lead, Contributor, or Reviewer). The role is automatically inferred — the project's project lead is set as Lead, everyone else as Contributor — but you can change it at any time.

Tip: For auto-fill to work, the project must have a size set (S, M, L, or XL) in the project editor. If no size is set, you'll be prompted to add one. Admins can adjust the default percentages per size and role in the Settings tab under "Allocation Defaults by Project Size."

Forecast Tab

The Forecast tab projects team capacity into the future based on current allocation patterns. It shows how loaded each person is over a configurable window (default 13 weeks from the current week), helping managers identify periods of over- or under-capacity and plan resource assignments accordingly.

Capacity Planner

At the top of the Forecast tab, the Capacity Planner answers the question: "When can each person take on a new project?" Select a project size (S/M/L/XL) and role (Lead, Contributor, or Reviewer) to see:

  • Earliest start date per team member — the first week where the person has sustained capacity for the full expected project duration without exceeding 100% utilization.
  • Timeline bars showing current utilization (green/yellow/red) with the proposed new project allocation overlaid in navy blue.
  • Blocking projects — which current projects are consuming the capacity that prevents an earlier start.

The planner uses the allocation defaults from Settings (e.g., a Medium project Lead needs 25% of project time) and the size-based expected durations (S=2 weeks, M=6 weeks, L=13 weeks, XL=26 weeks). It checks every future week to find the first point where the person can sustain that allocation for the full duration.

What the Forecast Shows

The Forecast tab has three main sections:

  • Summary Cards: One card per team member, sorted by most available. Each card shows the person's average free hours per week over the forecast window, their average utilization percentage, and a sparkline of weekly utilization.
  • Team Load Chart: An area chart showing the entire team's combined allocated hours vs. combined capacity over the forecast window. This reveals when the team as a whole is approaching capacity limits.
  • Utilization Heatmap: A detailed grid showing each person's utilization (or available hours) per week, color-coded from green (low utilization) through amber to red (at or over capacity). This makes it easy to spot who has capacity and when.

How Future Availability is Calculated

The forecast uses real allocation records first, with a "snapshot" fallback for weeks beyond where real data exists. Here's how it works step by step:

Step 1 — All Weeks: Real Data First

For all 52 weeks, the forecast uses actual allocation hours from the allocations service wherever they exist. When you create an Active or Scheduled project with auto-filled allocations, those real records are used directly — no estimation needed for those weeks.

Step 2 — Find the Snapshot Week

For each person, the system walks backward from the current week to find the most recent week where any allocations were entered (i.e., total allocation fractions across all projects are greater than zero). This becomes the person's "snapshot week."

For example, if James has allocation data entered through March 8, then March 8 is his snapshot week — even if the current calendar week is March 15.

Why a snapshot? The snapshot serves as a fallback for future weeks that have no real allocation records — for example, weeks beyond a project's end date range where auto-fill didn't create records, or for projects created before the auto-fill feature existed.

Step 3 — Fill Gaps with Snapshot

For future weeks only, if a project has no real allocation record for a given week but had a non-zero fraction in the snapshot week, the system fills that gap by projecting the snapshot fraction forward. Real records always take precedence — the snapshot never overwrites existing data.

Gap-filled Hours = Snapshot Fraction × Project Capacity for that week

Note that project capacity can vary week to week due to absences — a future vacation week will show reduced capacity (and therefore reduced allocated hours) if the absence has been entered in the Settings tab.

Step 4 — Respect Project End Dates

If a project has an end date and a future week falls after that date, the allocation drops to zero for that project. This is how the forecast shows capacity "freeing up" when projects end — revealing future availability for new work.

Step 5 — Calculate Availability

For each person, for each week:

Availability = Project Capacity − Total Allocated Hours

Where Project Capacity uses the standard formula: (Scheduled Hours − Absence Hours) × 0.75 × Project Time %.

Utilization is then: Total Allocated Hours ÷ Project Capacity, capped at 125% for display purposes.

Worked Example

James McGinnis is 63% project-allocated with no absences. In his snapshot week (March 8), he has:

ProjectSnapshot Fraction
Safe Cities Dashboard33%
Parking Utilization Analysis25%
Water Quality Mapping20%

For a future week with no absences, his project capacity is 18.9 hours. The forecast calculates:

ProjectCalculationHours
Safe Cities Dashboard0.33 × 18.96.24h
Parking Utilization0.25 × 18.94.73h
Water Quality Mapping0.20 × 18.93.78h
Total Allocated14.74h

Availability = 18.9 − 14.74 = 4.16 hours free. Utilization = 14.74 ÷ 18.9 = 78%.

If the Water Quality Mapping project ends in two weeks, then from that point forward James's forecast would show only 10.97h allocated and 7.93h free (58% utilization) — making him a good candidate for new work.

Limitations

Important: The forecast is a projection, not a plan. Keep these limitations in mind:
  • No allocation data = fully available: If a person has never had allocations entered (snapshot week is -1), their future shows as 100% available since there's nothing to project forward.
  • Future absences must be pre-entered: The forecast uses the capacity formula, which depends on absence data. If someone has a planned vacation next month but the absence hours haven't been entered in the Settings tab yet, the forecast won't reflect that reduced capacity.
  • Static snapshot: The forecast doesn't account for planned changes in allocation. If you know someone will move from Project A to Project B next month, the forecast won't reflect this until you actually update their allocations.
  • No new project predictions: The forecast only projects existing allocations forward — it can't predict when new projects will start consuming capacity.

Capacity & Utilization: Assumptions and Math

This section explains how the application calculates project capacity, allocated hours, and utilization. Understanding these calculations is important for interpreting the data on the Resources and Forecast tabs.

Core Assumptions

AssumptionValue / Explanation
Scheduled Work HoursComputed from each employee's daily start/end times and lunch duration: daily_hours = (end − start) − lunch, summed for the week. A 5/8 schedule (8am–5pm, 1hr lunch) yields 40 hours/week. A 9/80 schedule alternates between ~44 hours (Week A) and ~36 hours (Week B) across a 2-week pay period starting on Sundays.
Productivity Factor75% — Only 75% of available work hours are assumed to be productive project time. The remaining 25% accounts for meetings, email, administrative tasks, context-switching, and breaks. This is based on workplace productivity research showing the average knowledge worker is productively focused for roughly 6 of 8 hours per day.
Project Time %Each team member has a configurable percentage of their productive time dedicated to project work (e.g., 63% for a Lead Data Analyst). The remainder goes to operational duties, support, and overhead not tracked as projects.
AbsencesHours off per week (vacation, sick, holidays) are entered in the Settings tab. These reduce the scheduled hours before the productivity factor is applied.

The Capacity Formula

For each person, for each week:

Project Capacity = (Scheduled Hours − Absence Hours) × 0.75 × Project Time %

Where Scheduled Hours is determined by the employee's work schedule and which week of the pay period it is (Week A or Week B for 9/80 employees).

Worked Example — 5/8 Schedule, No Absences

James McGinnis has a 5/8 schedule, a project time allocation of 63%, and no absences this week:

StepCalculation
Scheduled hours40 hours (5/8 schedule)
Subtract absences40 − 0 = 40 hours available
Apply 75% productivity40 × 0.75 = 30 productive hours
Apply 63% project time30 × 0.63 = 18.9 project hours

If James (5/8 schedule) takes 8 hours (1 day) off:

Allocated Hours

When a team member is allocated a percentage to a project for a given week:

Allocated Hours = Allocation % × Project Capacity

For example, if James is allocated 50% to a project with 18.9 hours capacity: 0.50 × 18.9 = 9.45 hours. The total allocated hours for a person is the sum across all their project allocations for that week.

Utilization

Utilization measures how much of a person's available project capacity is actually allocated:

Utilization = Total Allocated Hours ÷ Project Capacity

Utilization is displayed as a percentage. 100% means fully allocated. Over 100% indicates overallocation. The color coding is:

  • Green — Under 70%
  • Amber — 70–90%
  • Red — Over 90%
Note: Both weekly_allocated and utilization are always computed client-side from the actual allocation data. This means they are always in sync with the latest edits, even before a page refresh.

Data Architecture

All data is stored in ArcGIS Online as hosted feature services. The application queries these services via the ArcGIS REST API using the authenticated user's OAuth token.

Week Structure

The application uses a 52-week calendar year (2026) where each week starts on Sunday. Week dates are stored as Date Only fields in YYYY-MM-DD format. The week of January 4, 2026 is Week 1.

Data Flow

On page load, the application queries projects, tasks, team_members, absences, allocations, and app_config in parallel. The data is reconstructed into an in-memory structure used for all rendering. If the logged-in user has time tracking enabled, their time entries are also loaded. When changes are made (editing allocations, updating absences, managing team members, logging time), the application writes back to the appropriate REST service and updates local data immediately for a responsive UI.

Access Control & Permissions

Authentication

All users must authenticate through ArcGIS Online OAuth 2.0 (Implicit Grant flow, 120-minute token lifetime). Expired tokens trigger automatic re-authentication.

Group-Based Permissions

Administrative privileges (Settings tab access, Idea promotion) are controlled by membership in a designated ArcGIS Online group (Team Leads). This is managed entirely through ArcGIS Online group membership — no application-level user management is needed.

Feature Service Permissions

Read and write access to the underlying feature services is controlled through ArcGIS Online sharing settings. The following services must have editing enabled: projects, tasks, team_members, absences, allocations, app_config, and time_entries.
